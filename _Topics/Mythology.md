**Mythology is never a passive collection of ancient fables; across these sources, it is shown to be an active, weaponized instrument of statecraft, psychological warfare, and military mobilization.** Ruling regimes, revolutionary vanguards, and intelligence networks systematically construct, distort, and deploy mythic narratives to provide spiritual justification for violence, sanitize historical defeats, and manipulate the loyalties of both domestic populations and foreign observers.

---

### 1. The Medieval Myth: The "Second Reconquista" and the Crusader Self-Image

In Nationalist Spain, General Francisco Franco and traditionalist elites did not merely fight a modern political war; they systematically resurrected the mythology of the **medieval Reconquista** to sanitize their military uprising.

- **The Crusading Heritage:** Traditionalists viewed the 1492 defeat of the Moors by Ferdinand and Isabella as both the culmination of a holy crusade and the genesis of Spanish civilization. By invoking this myth, the Nationalist alliance of 1936 cast its own campaign as a **"second Reconquista,"** projecting modern liberals, "reds," and regional separatists into the mythic role of the "contemporary heathen".
- **Liturgical Statecraft:** This mythic framing was reinforced through elaborate state rituals. Following his victory, Franco entered churches under canopies reserved for kings, laying his victorious sword before the miraculous **Christ of Lepanto**. Writers utilized this imagery to assure different factions of their own desires: for Carlists, it meant a Catholic dictatorship; for the Church, ecclesiastical supremacy; and for landowners, the divine preservation of their wealth.
- **Abstracting Atrocities:** By framing the civil war as a holy crusade, the regime successfully depersonalized its violence. Carlist _requeté_ militias, driven by a "medieval crusading faith," were explicitly told they would receive **a year less in purgatory for every red they killed**, reducing political slaughter to a transaction of spiritual duty.

### 2. The Martyrdom Myth: The Siege of the Alcázar

When raw military events are insufficient to inspire a population, politicized networks will aggressively embellish them to construct a **myth of heroic martyrdom**.

- **Biblical and Historical Parallelism:** The Nationalist defense of the Alcázar of Toledo—and the tragic story of the commandant's son, Luis Moscardó, who was supposedly executed by the left after refusing to surrender—was immediately elevated to a sacred myth. The Nationalist press enrobed the incident with **mystical implications of Abraham and Isaac (and even God and Christ)**, while drawing dramatic parallels to historical figures like Philip II handing his son over to the Inquisition.
- **The Reality vs. The Myth:** This mythology was highly manufactured. While the press extolled the "young cadets" of the military academy to cultivate a romantic, upper-class military mystique, the academy was actually on summer holiday. The grueling, courageous defense was in reality conducted by members of the far less glamorous, local **Civil Guard**.

### 3. Foundational Myths of Defeat: The "Ice March"

In the wreckage of the Russian Civil War, the anti-Bolshevik White armies required a powerful mythic anchor to bind their fragmented forces and justify their continued struggle.

- **The Genesis of White Identity:** The grueling **"Ice March"** (the First Kuban Campaign) of General Mikhail Alekseev’s Volunteer Army, alongside General Mikhail Drozdovsky’s 1,200-kilometer march from the Romanian front to Novocherkassk, was elevated into a foundational epic.
- **The Function of the Epic:** In the absence of a cohesive political program or administrative competence, these tales of extreme physical endurance, isolation, and survival became the **primary ideological glue** for the White movement, transforming a series of chaotic, defensive retreats into a legendary saga of chivalric, patriotic duty.

### 4. Left-Wing and Soviet Myth-Making: Marty and Kronstadt

The Left was equally adept at constructing mythologies, utilizing both historical legends and state-sponsored media to enforce military discipline and party authority.

- **The Legend of André Marty:** The absolute, unchecked authority of André Marty—the Comintern's ruthless controller of the International Brigades—was heavily sustained by the **"heroic legend"** woven around his role in the 1919 mutiny of the French Black Sea fleet. This party mythology made Marty practically untouchable, allowing his conspiracy complexes and purges of "Trotskyist-Fascist" spies to go entirely unchallenged.
- **Cinematic Re-Writing of History:** The Soviet state utilized revolutionary cinema to replace historical reality with convenient myths. Yefim Dzigan's celebrated film _**The Sailors of Kronstadt**_ depicted the transformation of anarchist sailors from the Kronstadt naval base into a highly disciplined, obedient unit of the Red Army. This cinematic myth was highly disorienting and deeply resented by real-world anarchists, who were fully aware of the historical reality: the Bolsheviks had ruthlessly and bloodily crushed the actual Kronstadt uprising.

### 5. Cold War Fabrications: Fictional Resistances

During the height of the Cold War, the CIA and Western psychological operations constructed elaborate myths to sustain the morale of populations trapped behind the Iron Curtain.

- **The Mirage of Liberation:** To stimulate "a spirit of non-cooperation" in Hungary, the CIA's Radio Free Europe (RFE) and balloon campaigns systematically promoted the myth of an active, highly organized underground resistance.
- **The Fictional Vanguard:** RFE broadcasted stories of entirely fictional domestic resistance groups like **"White Hand"** or **"Black Eagle"**. They even manufactured the myth of a **"Colonel Bell of the CIA,"** who repeatedly broadcasted pledges that the day of Western-backed liberation was imminent. This psychological myth-making ultimately proved tragic; when the 1956 revolution erupted, Hungarian fighters continued to sacrifice themselves in a hopeless struggle, operating under the deeply believed myth that Western military aid was on the way.
