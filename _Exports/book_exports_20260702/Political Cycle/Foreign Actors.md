Both Spain and Kansas experienced periods where their internal struggles were completely overtaken by the agendas of out-of-state actors. In both cases, these regions were transformed into **proxy battlegrounds** where larger, external powers fought for ideological dominance, political control, and economic gain.

Here is an analysis of how out-of-state influence manifested across both regions:

**1. Proxy Warfare and Military Interference** In both Spain and Kansas, external actors hijacked local conflicts to wage broader wars.

- **Spain** was turned into a **"world war by proxy"** by Nazi Germany, Fascist Italy, and the Soviet Union, who used the Spanish Civil War as a testing ground for modern weaponry and tactics. Germany and Italy provided critical airlifts and troops to General Franco, while the Soviet Union supplied conditional aid to the Republic and the Comintern organized the International Brigades.
- **Kansas** similarly became a proxy battleground for the American struggle over slavery. The state was subjected to guerrilla warfare driven by out-of-state "Border Ruffians" from Missouri, Confederate forces, and Indian tribes encouraged by the Confederacy to attack settlers. Furthermore, federal authorities in Washington D.C. bypassed Kansas's own governor to control its military organization, with out-of-state politicians building personal military machines that left the state vulnerable.

**2. Political Subversion and Ideological Control** External forces heavily manipulated the internal politics of both regions to align with their own ideologies.

- **In Spain**, the Soviet Union used its military aid to import Stalinist terror, infiltrating the Republic's military and secret police to ruthlessly dismantle independent leftist factions. Meanwhile, Western democracies crippled the Republic through an arms embargo, even as American and British corporations blatantly financed and supplied Franco's Nationalist forces.
- **In Kansas**, Eastern politicians and abolitionists tightly controlled the formation of the local Republican Party. National figures like Horace Greeley and Abraham Lincoln directly intervened to shape political platforms. Eastern financiers also funneled campaign money into Kansas and even manipulated humanitarian famine relief, distributing it disproportionately to ensure the election of their preferred candidates to the U.S. Senate.

**3. Severe Economic Exploitation** In both conflicts, the vulnerable local governments were mercilessly exploited for profit by the very external powers claiming to assist them.

- **Spain** was forced to mortgage its future. The Republic surrendered 510 tons of its gold reserves to Moscow to pay for Soviet arms, while the Soviets manipulated exchange rates to squeeze out profits. Concurrently, Germany established monopolies to extract Spanish mineral wealth to feed the Nazi rearmament program, and Hermann Göring even secretly sold obsolete weapons to the opposing Republic at a massive markup.
- **Kansas** faced a similarly predatory environment from out-of-state speculators. When the nascent state government desperately tried to sell bonds to fund itself, out-of-state agents colluded with federal officials in Washington. Through a corrupt scheme, they forced Kansas to sell its bonds at just 60 cents on the dollar, only to secretly resell them to the Federal government at 85 cents, pocketing the massive difference at the expense of the struggling state.

Ultimately, the theme of out-of-state influence in both Spain and Kansas reveals how **vulnerable regions can be stripped of their autonomy** when external powers decide to use them as staging grounds for their own military, political, and financial ambitions.
