### 1. Strategic Foundations: Active Defense and Abstraction

**1.1. The Philosophy of Active Defense** In the virtual theater of operations, the doctrine of **Active Defense** is a strategic posture where defense is the primary state, yet it is executed via tactical offense. This framework rejects passive containment. Instead, it utilizes a "defense-in-depth" and "whole-of-government" approach (Source para 1-28). While the grand strategy is defensive—protecting sovereign borders and settlement zones—the Lead Architect must be prepared to "fire the first shot" on the tactical plane (Source para 1-33). If an AI civilization encroaches on your projected State-Space, a preemptive tactical strike is required to maintain strategic integrity. This is a Zero-Sum calculation: any tile conceded to an opponent’s influence is a direct reduction of your own potential power.

**1.2. Computational Abstraction of National Power** To simplify complex game-state variables, players must use **Abstraction** to define **Comprehensive National Power (CNP)**. This metric allows for a Minimax evaluation—minimizing the opponent's potential while maximizing your own.

|   |   |   |
|---|---|---|
|Real-World CNP Component|Game Variable (Civilization 1)|Functional Application|
|**Hard Power**|Military Units (Phalanx, Chariot)|Direct kinetic coercion and border sanctity.|
|**Hard Power**|Gold (Treasury / GPT)|Mobilization readiness and "Rush Build" capacity.|
|**Soft Power**|Science (Research Rate)|Heuristic advantage through technological lethality.|
|**Soft Power**|Population (Total Citizens)|Human capital for industrial potential and drafting.|
|**Soft Power**|Wonders of the World|Strategic "Sanctuary" and global prestige indicators.|

**1.3. Political-Military Integration (Civil-Military Fusion)** The doctrine of Civil-Military Fusion mandates the synchronization of "Development" and "Security." To optimize this fusion, players must leverage civilian assets for military ends (Source para 1-13).

- **Instruction:** Utilize Settlers not merely for city-founding, but to construct strategic road networks toward the front lines. This "Dual-Purpose Infrastructure" accelerates the Strike System's deployment speed and ensures rapid reinforcement, effectively merging economic expansion with operational logistics.

### 2. Operational Environment: Pattern Recognition of the Map

**2.1. Terrain and Strategic Trends** Using **Pattern Recognition**, the Lead Architect identifies "High-Value Operational Environments" through the lens of A2/AD (Anti-Access/Area Denial).

- **Coastal Chokepoints:** These are primary interdiction zones. Do not merely block tiles; apply the principle of "defeating the buildup of combat power at extended ranges" (Source para 1-38). Deploy Sails/Frigates to sink enemy Transports in the global commons before they can establish a landing bridgehead.
- **Resource-Dense River Tiles:** Tactical hubs for trade-connectedness and rapid population scaling.
- **Mountain/Hill Buffers:** Sanctuary zones that provide a 50% defensive multiplier, functioning as natural fortifications for "Active Defense."

**2.2. Threat Classification** Threats must be classified by scale and objective, adhering to the Russian Military Doctrine framework (Source para 8):

- **Military Risk:** Factors indicating a potential threat. Example: A single Barbarian unit or a wandering AI Scout near borders.
- **Local War:** A conflict pursuing limited objectives, such as border friction or competition for a specific resource tile. Military actions stay within local borders and do not target the enemy capital (Source para 8f).
- **Regional War:** A war involving several states in the same region pursuing important military-political objectives (Source para 8g).
- **Large-Scale War:** A conflict against major coalitions pursuing radical objectives (capital capture). This requires the "mobilization of all physical resources" (Source para 8h).

### 3. System Warfare: Decomposing the Enemy Civilization

**3.1. Decomposition of the Enemy System** The AI opponent is an operational system that must be **Decomposed** into five critical subsystems for targeted degradation:

- **Command System:** The Capital city and Palace (The brain of the polity).
- **Strike System:** Offensive units like Chariots, Knights, and Catapults.
- **Information System:** The sensor network (Fog-of-war coverage provided by scouts).
- **Intelligence System:** The "Power Graph" and diplomatic contact points used by the AI to assess the player.
- **Support System:** The physical infrastructure (Irrigation, Mines, and Road networks).

**3.2. Asymmetric Targeting Stratagems**

1. **Support Degradation:** Use Horsemen to pillage irrigation. This is a system-level attack causing starvation, reducing the city's Soft Power (Population).
2. **Information Blinding:** Target and eliminate lone enemy scouts to reset the AI’s fog-of-war, denying them the "Informationized" view of your troop movements.
3. **Command Isolation:** Pillage roads connecting the Capital to peripheral cities to disrupt the Command System’s ability to send reinforcements.
4. **Intelligence Manipulation:** Deliberately gift low-tier technologies to a weaker AI to keep them in a "Regional War" with a stronger rival, manipulating the "Intelligence System" perceptions.
5. **Strike Interdiction:** Use ZOC (Zone of Control) to trap the enemy’s Strike System units in unfavorable terrain, exhausting their movement points before they reach your borders.

### 4. The Three Warfares in a Virtual Context

**4.1. Psychological and Public Opinion Warfare** This is the art of "winning without fighting" (Source para 1-31).

- **Public Opinion Warfare:** Use the Diplomacy screen to set the terms of debate. Demand "Withdrawal" to frame the AI as the aggressor.
- **Psychological Warfare:** Use the Power Graph as a tool of deterrence. Maintaining a high Hard Power score forces the AI into a submissive posture through the "multiplicative effect" of visible strength (Source para 1-59).

**4.2. Legal Warfare (Casus Belli)** Military measures must only be taken after non-violent instruments are exhausted (Source para 5).

- **Algorithm:**
    1. Request AI withdrawal from territory.
    2. Demand tribute/technology as "reparations" for border friction.
    3. If negotiations fail, you have established the "legal basis" for operations (Source para 1-60).
    4. Only then transition the government to Monarchy/Despotism (Martial Law) to maximize unit support and suppress the unhappiness typical of "Legal" constraints in Republics.

### 5. Tactical Execution: Algorithms for Maneuver

**5.1. People’s War Principles for Sieges** Apply the following **Step-by-Step Computational Algorithm** for city conquest:

1. **IF** the target city possesses "City Walls," **THEN** deploy Horsemen/Knights to "Pillage" all Support System tiles (Irrigation).
2. **WAIT** for city-level starvation to shrink the population, thereby reducing the unit-support capacity.
3. **IF** defender strength is > 1/3 of attacker strength, **THEN** wait for Catapult arrival.
4. **ELSE**, execute the "Storming Attack" (Source Fig 7-15), concentrating forces for an overwhelming 3:1 numerical advantage.
5. **CAPTURE** the urban hub and immediately fortify a Phalanx to "Consolidate" the position (Source Fig 8-6).

**5.2. Joint Operations and Precision Strike** Coordinate land and sea forces to establish **Sanctuary Zones**. Use naval units to clear coastlines (Precision Strike) while ground forces (Knights) establish a "Bridgehead." This "Integrated Employment of Force" ensures your Strike System is not interdicted during the transition from sea to land.

**5.3. Informationized Reconnaissance**

- **Linear Search:** Coastal mapping with Triremes to define the edge of the operational theater.
- **Area Search:** Expanding spiral movement from the capital to identify resource-dense river tiles.
- **Screening:** Placing inexpensive Militia on the empire's periphery as early-warning sensors to detect "Military Risks."

### 6. Mobilization and Evaluation

**6.1. Readiness and Strategic Deployment Checklist** Before escalating to a "Large-Scale War," conduct this audit:

- **Human Resources:** Is the population sufficient to sustain production/drafting losses?
- **Logistical Resources:** Is the Treasury sufficient for 10+ turns of "Rush Building" replacements?
- **Industrial Potential:** Are core cities optimized for "Shield" production over "Science" development?

**6.2. Continuous Evaluation (Heuristic Audit of the Game State)** Perform this audit every five turns to assess success indicators and required adjustments.

|---|---|---|
