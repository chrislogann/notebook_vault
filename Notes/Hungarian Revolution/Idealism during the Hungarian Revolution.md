- **Euphoric and Utopian "People Power":** The 1956 Hungarian Revolution was characterized by early "idealism and breathless excitement" . Long, spontaneous revolutionary meetings were defined by "intoxicating" words of liberty and hope, though the endlessly debated resolutions grew increasingly unrealistic as citizens became "drunk with the joy of the present" .
- **Individual Acts of Heroic Idealism:** The passionate, artistic young revolutionary Gabor Földes—a theater director and idealist—demonstrated extreme moral courage in Mosonmagyarovar when he risked his own life to protect captured secret police (AVO) officers from being brutally lynched by a furious, vengeful crowd .
