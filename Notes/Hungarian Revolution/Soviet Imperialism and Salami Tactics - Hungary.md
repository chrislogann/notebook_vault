In post-WWII Hungary, the intersection of business and politics took the form of rapid state-sanctioned nationalization and colonial exploitation by the Soviet Union:

- **Salami Tactics and Nationalization:** Mátyás Rákosi, the Communist leader of Hungary, openly boasted about using "salami tactics" to slice away democratic opposition and seize the economy. Rákosi explained: _"Our demands were always modest at first—and were then increased... first we demanded only 'government control' of the banks; only later did we call for the outright nationalisation of the three largest banks"_.
- **Colonial Resource Extraction:** The Soviet Union treated Hungary as a classic colonial possession, pillaging its vast uranium reserves at Kővágószőllős (near Pécs) under the guise of a secret joint company codenamed "Bauxite". The terms of the deal were incredibly lopsided: Hungary was forced to build the infrastructure and give the sites to the Soviets tax-free, while Moscow maintained a total monopoly, setting the purchase price unilaterally and keeping the operation shrouded in such immense secrecy that even Hungary’s own Atomic Energy Commission was kept entirely in the dark.
