- **Neo-Soviet Capitalism and the Nomenklatura:** In modern Russia, a vast bureaucratic class (the _nomenklatura_) uses state power to extort continuous bribes from small and middle-sized businesses to support their high-class lifestyles. Successful business owners like Tanya **realistically accept that to survive and acquire property, they must routinely pay off local police, firemen, and hygiene inspectors**, viewing these bribes as a necessary "tax" in a system of gangster capitalism.
- **Hostile Takeovers and Judicial Complicity:** The formal rule of law is routinely bypassed by the realistic imperative of political and corporate expediency. Under **"telephone law" (****pozvonochnost’****), state and municipal officials call judges directly to dictate required verdicts**, allowing corrupt judges like Ryazantsev and Balashov to rubber-stamp the hostile, armed takeovers of valuable private enterprises—such as the Kachkanar OEC and the Uralkhimmash Corporation.
- **The State-Directed Destruction of Yukos:** Putin’s systematic destruction of the Yukos oil giant and the jailing of its CEO, Mikhail Khodorkovsky, was a direct, ruthless retaliation for Khodorkovsky politically challenging the Kremlin and financing democratic opposition parties. Once the company was broken, **the state immediately positioned loyal bureaucrat Muhammed Tsikanov in the parent company to coordinate the transfer of Yukos's multi-billion-dollar assets into the hands of favored Kremlin loyalists**.
