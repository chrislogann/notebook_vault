- **The Spirit of Democratic Reform in the Courts:** Despite the Russian judicial system's total subservience to the executive, the spirit of democratic reform historically survived in the idealistic, principled work of legal figures like Judge Bukreev and Prosecutor Vershinin, who attempted to uphold the law against political expediency .
- **Unrecognized, Selfless Patriotism:** In isolated, severely impoverished provincial garrisons like Rybachie, military personnel like Captain Dikiy demonstrated an enduring, selfless patriotism . They continued to work devotedly and lovingly under troglodytic, underfunded conditions, in spite of a state administration that showed a "pathological lack of respect" for their service .
- **A Humanist Journalistic Mission:** Anna Politkovskaya's own writing was driven by an idealistic focus on ordinary human lives . Rejecting the loud and cynical ideologues of both sides in the Chechen conflict, she dedicated her work to telling the deeply personal stories of "brave, kind, and honest ordinary people" .
