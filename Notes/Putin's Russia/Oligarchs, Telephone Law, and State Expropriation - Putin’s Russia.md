In modern Russia, the economic system represents a hybrid of a free market, Soviet-style bureaucratic control (_nomenklatura_), and various features designed to put "Soviet ideology at the service of big-time private capital".

- **The Necessity of State "Curators":** In Putin's Russia, _"big business without patrons, or 'curators,' in the state administration does not exist"_. To survive, businesses must pay constant bribes to a vast bureaucratic class that has taken corruption to heights undreamed of under previous regimes.
- **Hostile Takeovers and the UBOP:** Prominent Urals gangsters and oligarchs, like Pavel Fedulev, systematically seized major state assets—such as the Kachkanar Ore Enrichment Complex (OEC) and the Uralkhimmash Corporation—using armed groups, baseball bats, and the complicity of the local police. These seizures were protected by high-ranking police officials (such as Yekaterinburg police chief Nikolai Ovchinnikov, who was later promoted to lead the federal anti-organized crime agency GUBOP).
- **"Telephone Law" (****Pozvonochnost’****):** To formalize these hostile takeovers, oligarchs relied on a highly corrupt judiciary. Judges in Sverdlovsk Province issued rapid, highly biased "rulings-to-order" dictated to them over the telephone by political and business patrons. Corrupt judges like Judge Balashov regularly rubber-stamped illegal property transfers and issued injunctions to block legitimate shareholders' meetings.
- **The Expropriation of Yukos:** When business interests clashed directly with Putin’s personal power, the state utilized its full punitive apparatus. Mikhail Khodorkovsky, the chief executive of the highly transparent oil giant Yukos, made the mistake of donating to the country’s democratic opposition parties (Yabloko and the Union of Right Forces) and politically challenging Putin. In retaliation, Putin jailed Khodorkovsky and dismantled Yukos, ultimately parachuting in a loyal bureaucrat (Muhammed Tsikanov) to coordinate the delivery of the company’s multi-billion-dollar assets into the hands of those favored by the Kremlin.
