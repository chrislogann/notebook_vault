[[Role of Foreign Actors]]

Out-of-state actors fundamentally transformed the Spanish Civil War from an internal military coup into almost a **"world war by proxy,"** using Spain as a testing ground for modern weaponry, a political chessboard, and a distraction for broader geopolitical ambitions. Foreign intervention shaped the conflict in four major ways: military aid, political subversion, economic exploitation, and diplomatic isolation.

**Axis Military Intervention and the "Testing Ground"** Nazi Germany and Fascist Italy provided the critical military lifeline that ensured the survival and ultimate victory of General Franco's Nationalist forces.

- **The Initial Airlift:** Hitler and Mussolini provided Junkers 52 and Savoia-Marchetti transport planes to initiate the first major military airlift in history, ferrying the elite Army of Africa from Morocco to the Spanish mainland.
- **Testing Tactics for WWII:** Germany used the war to test its rapidly developing military technology and _blitzkrieg_ tactics. The Luftwaffe's Condor Legion experimented with the new Stuka dive-bomber, carpet-bombing techniques, and the use of 88mm anti-aircraft guns against tanks. They methodically tested the psychological effects of aerial terrorism on civilians, most infamously during the devastating carpet-bombing of the Basque town of Guernica.
- **Troop Deployments:** Italy sought imperial glory in the Mediterranean by deploying the _Corpo di Truppe Volontarie_ (CTV), which eventually totaled around 50,000 men.

**Soviet Military Aid and Communist Subversion** The Soviet Union was the only major power to supply the legitimately elected Spanish Republic with weapons, but this aid came with severe political strings that fractured the Republic's internal unity.

- **Conditional Military Support:** Stalin authorized the sale of modern T-26 and BT-5 tanks, as well as fighter aircraft, but he sent just enough to maintain the Republic's resistance without provoking a broader war with Hitler. By 1938, as Stalin shifted his geopolitical focus toward an eventual pact with Nazi Germany, Soviet interest and arms shipments dwindled.
- **The International Brigades:** The Communist International (Comintern) recruited and organized between 32,000 and 35,000 foreign volunteers from 53 countries to fight for the Republic, utilizing them as elite shock troops in major battles.
- **Importing the Stalinist Terror:** Soviet military advisers and NKVD operatives, led by Aleksander Orlov, infiltrated the Republican army and took over its secret police (the SIM). They imported the paranoia of Stalin's show trials, ruthlessly hunting down "Trotskyists" and dismantling independent leftist factions like the anarchists (CNT) and the POUM. This culminated in the NKVD's torture and murder of the POUM leader Andreu Nin.

**The Farce of Western "Non-Intervention"** While the Axis powers blatantly supplied Franco, the Western democracies—primarily Britain, France, and the United States—adopted a policy of appeasement that fatally crippled the Republic.

- **The Arms Embargo:** Driven by a fear of Bolshevism and a desperate desire to avoid a broader European war, Britain pushed France into forming the Non-Intervention Committee. This embargo prevented the Spanish Republic from buying arms on the open market but entirely failed to stop massive German and Italian shipments to the Nationalists.
- **Corporate Backing for Franco:** Despite official neutrality, American and British business interests heavily financed and supplied Franco. The Texas Oil Company (Texaco) and Standard Oil provided the Nationalists with 3,500,000 tons of oil on credit, while Ford, Studebaker, and General Motors supplied 12,000 trucks—nearly three times as many vehicles as the Axis powers provided.

**Economic Exploitation** Both the Nationalists and the Republic paid heavily for foreign aid, mortgaging Spain's future to out-of-state actors.

- **The Gold Reserves:** To pay for Soviet arms, the Republican government was convinced to transfer 510 tons of the Bank of Spain's gold reserves (worth over $518 million) to Moscow. The Soviets utilized highly manipulated exchange rates to squeeze a considerable profit out of the desperately besieged Republic.
- **German Monopolies:** In exchange for Condor Legion support, Hermann Göring demanded payment in Spanish mineral wealth. Germany created the HISMA/ROWAK monopoly, and later the Montana Project, to extract iron, mercury, pyrites, and tungsten from Spanish mines to feed the Nazi rearmament program. In a shocking paradox, Göring also used a front company to secretly sell obsolete weapons at massive markups to the Spanish Republic, profiting off both sides of the war.
