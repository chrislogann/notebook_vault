- **Statecraft Free of Personal Ambition:** During the Wyandotte Constitutional Convention, the voters of Kansas deliberately elected politically inexperienced delegates—predominantly "common farmers" and members of the "great middle class"—under the idealistic expectation that these delegates had "no personal ambition to gratify, no animosities to resent, no friends to favor" .
- **An Uncompromising Defense of Human Rights:** During the convention's equal rights debates, delegates like S.D. Houston offered an uncompromising, idealistic defense of female suffrage and human liberties, declaring that "the time is coming when it will be impossible for any power in this land to curtail one single human right" .
